• How many versions of the Standard FX Trader's trading platform are available?

    Standard FX Trader clients have access to a web - HTML browser-based version and a Windows-based version of the dealing platform. The platform is a secure and robust environment in which to trade and manage an account.

    The Windows-based platform requires a once-off installation and provides tick by tick position management. The browser-based trading platform may be accessed at anytime and from anywhere with internet access. No download or installation is required.

    The platform tracks all trading activity, profit and loss and margin analysis and features an integrated charting tool, news and analysis on a single screen.

  • How do I clear the cache for Internet Explorer?

    Exit the browser by closing all open windows in Internet Explorer.

    Click on Start, then on Settings (Windows 98/2000), then the Control Panel.

    Click Internet Options.

    In the general tab section, click on Delete Files and then click OK.

    Click OK again to close the settings dialogue box.

  • How do I manage my currency pair subscriptions?

    To manage your rate subscriptions, login to your trading platform and click on ‘MyAccount’. Once there, click on ’My Profile’ > ’Add/Edit Currency Pairs’. Once there you can customise your Standard FX Trader platform to show only the pairs you are most interested in trading. Simply enable or disable streaming rates for any available currency pair. Your settings will be saved for future logins.

  • I do not see anything when I click on the logon button?

    You may have a pop-up blocker installed. The web version of Standard FX Trader launches itself into a new window, which some pop-up blockers may identify as a pop-up. For example, if you use Internet Explorer 6, the built-in pop-up blocker is turned on by default but can easily be disabled for a trusted site.

    To access the web version of Standard FX Trader with a pop-up blocker installed, you must either:

    Add the web version of Standard FX Trader to your list of allowed/trusted sites.

    Temporarily bypass the pop-up blocker.

    Please choose your pop-up blocker or browser type from the list below and follow the instructions:

    • Internet Explorer

      (If you use Internet Explorer with a Windows XP operating system, the recent Service Pack 2 upgrade for XP installed a pop-up blocker in your browser.)

      Choose from one of the following options to access the web version of Standard FX Trader:

      Add Standard FX Trader as an allowed site:

      Open Internet Explorer.

      On the Tools menu, point to Pop-up blocker, and then click Pop-up blocker settings.

      Internet Explorer Tools menu

      Type "demo.efxnow.com" in the Address of website to allow box.

      Click Add.

      Type "ssl.efxnow.com" in the Address of website to allow box.

      Click Add.

    • Toolbar pop-up blockers

      Temporarily bypass your pop up blocker:

      It is also possible to bypass the pop-up blocker on a case by case basis. Hold down the CTRL key while you click Login. Make sure to hold down the Ctrl key until the platform fully loads.

      To temporarily bypass pop-up blockers associated with toolbars, simply hold down the CTRL key while you click on "Login". Hold down the CTRL key until the platform fully loads.

    • Firefox

      Firefox has a built in pop-up blocker as part of its design. To allow pop-ups on trusted sites, open the browser:

      Click on the Tools tab.

      Select Options.

      Click on Web Features (at left).

      Click on Allowed sites.

      Type demo.efxnow.com and click on Allow.

      Type ssl.efxnow.com and click on Allow.

      Click OK.

    • Netscape 7.2

      To allow pop-ups from Standard FX Trader:

      Click on Tools.

      Click Pop-up Manager.

      Add "demo.efxnow.com" to the list of websites on which you would like to allow pop-ups.

      Add "ssl.efxnow.com" to the list of website on which you would like to allow pop-ups.

      This will add the site to your list and pop-ups will be allowed from then on any time you visit that page.

      Note: If you already have a site in your "allowed" list, but still cannot access it through "OneStop" - try entering the address for the site you need directly into the browsers address line

  • I cannot see rates in the Standard FX Trader for web platform?

    Several reasons could explain why you are not receiving rates on web version of Standard FX Trader.

    • Your internet security settings may be restricting you from receiving our rates.
    • You may need to install Microsoft Virtual Machine.
    • Your machine may be running an older version of Internet Explorer.
    • You may have a firewall that is restricting traffic over certain ports.
    • Please close all open Internet Explorer windows, re-launch Internet Explorer and follow the instructions below:

    1. Check your internet security settings

    "High" or "Custom Level" security setting will restrict you from receiving our rates.

    To modify your settings:

    • Click on Tools (adjacent to your Favorites).
    • Click on Internet Options.
    • Click on the Security tab.
    • Choose Default Level.
    • Click Apply.
    • Click OK.

    2. Install the Microsoft Virtual Machine (VM)

    After you do this, try to log into the trading platform. You should see a window that looks like this:

    Click on the "Run" button to install our software, and then you should be able to see rates. If you do not get this box, please continue onto step two.

    • The Microsoft VM is necessary to receive our rates; it is a software component that comes with earlier versions of Windows, but has been omitted from later versions. To install it, click on the link which says:
      Click here to install Microsoft VM.
    • After the download, which takes approximately one minute with a broadband connection, click Yes to install the software. You will then be prompted to restart your machine. If you see the box labelled in the screenshot above, but you still do not have the rates, continue onto step three.

    3. Update your copy of Internet Explorer to v5.0 or later

    • To check your version of Internet Explorer, click on Help/About Internet Explorer. The browser version will be on the first line.
    • To upgrade your version of Internet Explorer visit, http://www.microsoft.com/ie. This is a free download.
    • Click on the Install Internet Explorer link in the centre of the page.
    • Once the process is complete, restart your computer and try to log into the trading platform. If you still cannot see rates, continue to step four.

    4. Firewall modification

    Personal PC users:

    • If you have a firewall installed on your computer, it may be restricting traffic necessary for the platform to operate correctly. Firewalls may cause connectivity issues, affect access to the live rates and charting on the platform. If a firewall is installed, you will need to modify it to allow traffic on the following ports with a TCP protocol.

      Destination addresses and protocols:

      • Port 3020/1000
      • IP address
        • 67.151.132.41
        • 67.151.132.65
      • Port 1000
      • IP address
        • 202.161.164.227
        • 202.161.164.238
    • Most corporate users access the internet through a firewall. This is most likely the reason you cannot see our rates. Contact your network administrator if you wish to access our trading software via your corporate network. Please note we do not support operation behind a proxy at this time.

  • The trading platform did not automatically launch in my browser?

    • When you click on the login button, the Standard FX Trader trading platform is launched into a new window, which some pop-up blockers might identify as a pop-up. In order to view the trading platform, you will have to either disable your blocker software or add Standard FX Trader to the list of sites your software will allow pop-ups to originate from.
    • Sometimes a blocker may be installed without your knowledge. You may have to check your list of installed programmes and see if there is a programme listed that sounds like it could be a pop-up blocker. To do this, click on the Start button, and then click on Settings on Windows 98/2000, then click on Control Panel. Double-click on the Add/Remove Programmes icon, and it will display a list of the software on your computer.
    • After checking for pop-up blocking, you can click here to try logging into the platform again with the user ID and password you received in your email.
