  • Dealing spreads

    At Standard FX Trader, you can trade on dealing spreads as low as one to two pips on the most widely traded currency pairs. Our fractional pip pricing gives you the added advantage of using the extra digit to make the most of smaller price movements.

  • Trading minimums

    • A Mini Account
      The minimum transaction size for Mini Accounts is a 1/10th standard sized lot, or 10,000 of the base currency, with a minimum margin deposit of 0.5% (200:1 leverage). For example, a US$10,000 position would require an initial margin deposit of US$50. Fund your Mini Account with a minimum of US$250.
    • A Standard Account
      The minimum transaction size for Standard Accounts is one lot, or 100,000 of the base currency, with a minimum margin deposit of 1% (100:1 leverage). For example, a US$100,000 position would require an initial margin deposit of US$1,000. Fund your Standard Account with a minimum of US$2,500.

  • Fees

    Standard FX Trader charges no trading commissions or transaction fees. We charge no fees since our revenues are generated by the difference between the buy and sell price. Our clients can trade on spreads as tight as one to two pips on the most widely traded currency pairs.

    Learn more about Standard FX Trader's pricing and dealing spreads.

  • Price quotes

    Standard FX Trader clients have the ability to execute trades directly from real time streaming bid/ask quotes. Live prices are continuously published to clients via Standard FX Trader's trading platform, and traders can click on the current bid or offer at any time and instantaneously execute a trade. Prices are updated automatically as market conditions dictate. Approximately 100,000 transactions are currently priced daily on the system being utilised by Standard FX Trader.

  • Trading over the internet

    Executing a deal with Standard FX Trader via the internet is a simple two-step process.

    1. Simply enter the number of lots and then click on the bid (buy) or offer (sell) for the currency pair you wish to trade.
    2. Your deal is immediately executed. The dealing software automatically calculates the initial margin requirement based upon the notional amount of the deal, and if sufficient funds are available in your account, the transaction is accepted.

    Deals are confirmed online, usually within a few seconds, and the system instantaneously updates both your open position and calculates your current P&L.

  • Phone trading

    Live clients may, if necessary, trade over the telephone with local dealers from 08h00 to 17h00 (CAT or GMT+2), Mondays to Fridays. Outside of these hours, these calls will be routed to our experienced support team.

    All trades executed via the phone are subject to a pre-deal margin availability check and will be manually entered into the client's account for integrated P&L analysis and reporting. All telephone calls are recorded for the safety of both parties.

    Whilst we offer phone trading, this should, as much as possible, be utilised as an alternative only when absolutely necessary.

  • Phone dealing procedure

    1. Immediately state your user ID and a sales representative will validate your details.
    2. Clarify your interest. Always include the number of lots and the currency pair you are interested in. For example, "I would like a price on five (5) lots of Euro/dollar".
    3. The dealer will then provide a two-way price quote. For example, "Euro/dollar is 1.28551/562" (the first number being the bid, the second the offer).
    4. State your trade. For example, "At 1.28551, I sell five (5) lots of Euro/dollar" or "At 1.28562, I buy five (5) lots of Euro/dollar".
    5. If you do not wish to deal at the quoted levels, simply say "Nothing done," hang up the phone and call again later. Alternatively, you could place a limit or stop order at your desired level.
    6. Remember: the price given is the dealing price at that time; haggling is not permitted nor are traders allowed to remain on the phone until the price changes.
    7. It is important to remember that the dealing desk phone lines are reserved for dealing/order purposes only, and that proper phone dealing procedures should be observed at all times. All other inquiries, such as account issues or general information, can be addressed on live chat, by calling 0800 SPOT FX [or +27 (0)11 378 SPOT] or via email to standardfxtrader@standardbank.co.za.

  • Order types *

    • Market Order
      A market order is an order to buy or sell at the current market price.

    • Limit Order
      A limit order is an order to buy or sell at a specified price. Limit orders can be used to enter or exit into a position. A buy limit order can only be executed at the limit price or lower, and a sell limit order can only be executed at the limit price or higher.

    • Stop order
      A stop order is an order to buy or sell when a predefined order rate is reached. Stop orders can be used to enter or exit into a position. A buy stop is entered at a stop price that is always above the current market price. A sell stop order helps to avoid further losses or to protect a profit that exists if a stock price continues to drop. A stop order to sell is always placed below the current market price.

    • One Cancels Other (OCO's)
      A contingent order providing that one part of the order is cancelled if the other part is executed. If one part of the order is filled, the other is automatically cancelled.

    • If / Then
      An If/Then order provides that if the first order ("If" order) is executed, the second order ("Then" order) is activated as a live, single order. In cases where the "If" order does not execute, the "Then" single order will remain dormant. When either part of an If / Then order is cancelled, all parts of the order are cancelled as well.

    • If /Then OCO
      An If/Then OCO provides that if the first order ("If" order) is executed, the second order ("Then" order) is activated as a One Cancels Other (OCO) order. The execution of either one of the two "Then" orders automatically cancels the other.

      In cases where the 'If' single order does not execute, the "Then" OCO order will remain dormant. When any part of an If / Then OCO order is cancelled, including either leg of the OCO order, all parts of the order are cancelled as well.

    • Trailing Stops
      A trailing stop is an order that makes use of an automatically adjusted stop-loss limit instruction to maintain a trigger price. The trigger price dynamically follows the market price in the favorable direction at a specified distance. A trailing stop allows a trade to continue to gain in value when the market price moves in a favorable direction, but automatically closes the trade if the market price suddenly moves in an unfavorable direction by a specified distance. When the market price moves in a favorable direction (up for long positions, down for short positions), the trigger price follows the market price by the specified stop distance. If the market price moves in an unfavorable direction, the trigger price stays stationary and the distance between this price and the market price becomes smaller. If the market price continues to move in an unfavorable direction until it reaches the trigger price, an order is triggered to close the trade

    For step by step instructions on how to place orders on the trading platform, see our user guide.

    All of the above orders may be entered as Day Orders, entered today and good until the end of the New York business day 17h00 (ET) (23h00 CAT in US summers or 24h00 CAT in US winters). You may also choose to enter a Good til Cancelled Order (GTC), which is valid for 90 days or until the order is executed or cancelled. Orders remain open until they are triggered or cancelled. If you close out a position manually, you must cancel any order(s) relating to that position

    Orders remain open until they are triggered or cancelled. If a position is closed manually, any order(s) relating to that position must also be cancelled.

  • Order execution*

    Market orders
    Market orders are executed at the best available price at the time the order is received.

    Standard FX Trader platforms that support Market Orders include:

    • Standard FX Trader Windows
    • Website trading

    Instant Execution Mode
    Instant Execution is designed for use in combination with the one-click dealing feature available in the Standard FX Trader windows version, and is only available from the dealing boxes on the Standard FX Trader windows version trading platform. When Instant Execution is enabled, if the market moves unfavorably away from the requested price by more than1 pip, the order is not executed. However, if the market moves in the customer's favor by any amount then the order will be executed at the improved price. Limit Orders: Limit orders are executed at the order rate or better. Sell limit orders are filled when the bid reaches the requested rate; limit orders to buy are filled at the offer price.

    Stop Orders
    Stop orders become market orders when a predefined order rate is reached. Stop orders are executed at the best available price, depending on available liquidity. Buy Stop orders are filled on the offer price and Sell Stop orders are filled at the bid price.

    First In First Out (FIFO)
    Open positions are closed according to the (First in First Out) FIFO accounting rule. All trades opened within a particular currency pair are closed in the order in which they were originally opened.

    Orders left over the weekend or holidays
    The orders left pending at the close of trading on Friday at 17h00 (ET) (23h00 CAT in US summers or 24h00 CAT in US winters) or those placed over the weekend are subject to a gap open on Sunday evening when Standard FX Trader commences trading at 23h00 CAT in US summers or 24h00 CAT in US winters. In the case of both stop loss and limit orders - should your order be triggered due to news, events or other fundamental factors, it will not be executed over the weekend. Your order WILL be executed at the prevailing price when Standard FX Trader's trading desk opens again. Due to the additional gap risk involved, it is usually advisable to reconsider leaving open orders over the weekend or holidays.

    Order Expiry
    End of Day (EOD) orders automatically expire at 5pm EST on the same day the order was entered. Good 'til Cancelled (GTC) orders are valid for 90 days, and automatically expire on the Saturday following the 90th calendar day.

    Order execution in volatile/gap market conditions
    When economic data releases or major news events are announced, prices may "gap" as the market reacts and adjusts to the news. Market conditions can be extremely volatile during these times - prices may move dramatically in one direction and in some cases there may be no available liquidity for a short period. Gap market conditions are also common when trading resumes after a weekend or holiday. During gap market conditions, stop and limit orders may be executed at prices far away from the requested price. This deviation can be both in favor of and not in favor of the client. For maximum control over order execution, clients may employ Instant Execution mode.

    *Please note that placing contingent orders may not necessarily limit your losses

  • Margin

    The maximum available margin is 0.5% (a 200 x leverage) for Mini Accounts and 1% (a 100 x leverage) for Standard Accounts. Since higher leverage elevates both your profits and losses, traders can exercise the option of employing a lower degree of leverage at any time.

    The minimum margin requirement is approximately US$50 per lot in a Mini Account and approximately US$1000 per lot in a Standard Account. The requirements for leverage may vary with account size or market conditions, and may be changed from time to time at the sole discretion of Standard FX Trader.

    Consider this margin example:
    USD based currency pair

    Margin = (Contract size / Leverage)
    You have US$500 in a Mini Account. To calculate the margin required to execute eight (8) mini lots of USD/JPY (80,000 USD) at 200 x leverage, simply divide the deal size by the leverage amount (e.g. 80,000 / 200 = 400). You post US$400 margin for this trade, leaving a US$100 balance in your account.

    Non-USD Based Currency Pair

    Margin = [(Contract Size x Price) / Leverage]

    In this example, you have US$5,000 in a standard account. You want to execute three (3) lots of EUR/USD (300,000 Euros) at the current market price of 1.27100 using 100 x leverage. To calculate the required margin for this position, multiply the deal size (300,000) by the price (1.27100) and then divide by the leverage (100 x), (e.g. [(300,000*1.27100)/100] = US$3,813). After you executed this trade, you would have a balance of US$1,187 in your account.

    The Standard FX Trader trading platform automatically calculates margin requirements and checks available funds before allowing you to successfully enter a new position. If you do not have adequate funds to enter a new position, you will receive an "insufficient margin funds" message when attempting to deal. There is both a Margin Monitor as well as a Margin Calculator available on the Standard FX Trader platform to guide you.

    If the unrealised P&L of your net total open position falls below your margin balance, your account is considered under margined and all your open positions may be liquidated. To avoid liquidation of your positions, do not use your entire account balance as margin for open positions. Instead, leave enough funds in your account to withstand a market movement against your open positions. We suggest you always use stop loss orders in an attempt to limit your downside risk.

    Please contact a Standard FX Trader client services representative at any time should you wish to use a lower or higher degree of leverage or to otherwise adjust the margin settings in your account.

  • Rollovers

    Standard FX Trader clients have the opportunity to earn interest on positions left open overnight - at all margin levels. The potential amount of interest to be earned depends on the direction of the open position and the interest rate differential between the two currencies involved. For example, UK interest rates are significantly higher than Japan's, thus if a trader is long GBP/JPY (i.e. holding British pounds), interest can be earned on the roll. Conversely, if a trader is short GBP/JPY (i.e. holding yen) interest will be paid on the rollover.

    Standard FX Trader will automatically roll forward all open positions to the next day's value date following the close of New York trading at 17h00 (ET) (23h00 CAT in US summer and 24h00 CAT in US winters). Rollover credits or debits will be applied directly to customer account balances reflecting interest paid or earned on each open position held overnight.

  • Daily housekeeping

    Daily housekeeping will take place each evening at 17h00 (ET) (23h00 CAT in US summers or 24h00 CAT in US winters) and will last approximately five minutes. During that time, back office staff will conduct daily rolls and important system maintenance tasks will be performed. Online trading MAY be unavailable, but phone orders will still be accepted.

  • Confirmations

    Deals are confirmed on screen, typically within a few seconds. You can also view the full details of each transaction on screen, including the date, time, rate, and notional amount bought and sold, USD value, and reference number.

  • Reporting

    Standard FX Trader’s dealing software tracks all trading activity as it occurs, allowing its clients to view current open positions, real-time profit and loss, margin availability, account balances, and all historical transaction details directly on screen.

    In addition, by clicking on the ‘Reports’ tab on the menu bar, there are five ad hoc reports which you can access, including:

    1. Account value summary - an online monthly account statement. View your current account balance (realised P&L) for a selected month, together with all deposits, withdrawals, interest earned, and fees charged (if any).
    2. Detailed transaction listing - lists complete trade detail for any selected date range, including deal date, currency pair dealt, trade direction (buy or sell), contract size for both currencies in the pair dealt, and the executed deal rate.
    3. Open FX positions - shows a summary view of all open positions, including contract size, USD value, average rate of open positions, reval rate (current market rate), and unrealised P&L. This report supplements real-time position information available on the position management screen of the trading platform.
    4. Order history - provides detail on all order activity for a selected data range, including order entry date and time stamp, listing of all cancelled or executed orders, along with its reference number. The log entry column provides a confirmation number and action detail for any order.
    5. Rollover history - provides rollover details for any transaction held open past 17h00 (ET) (23h00 CAT in US summers and 24h00 CAT in US winters), including rollover rate and USD value. It can be generated for any given date range.

    Note: All reports can be printed or exported into Microsoft Excel by simply cutting and pasting the data.

  • Account statements

    Customer account statements are provided online in the ’Reports’ section of the trading platform. Customers have access to a full suite of available reports, including account value summary, detailed transaction listing, open positions, to name a few. Reports may be generated for any date range, and printed or saved for future reference.
