• What is the difference between a practice and live trading account?

    There is no capital at risk when trading in a practice account. In addition, a Standard FX Trader practice account features real-time quotes and charts, along with all the trading tools and information you will have access to as a full Standard FX Trader customer, including single and contingent order types, a real-time newsfeed, up-to-the-minute market commentary, daily and weekly research, and more.

  • How much money do I need to open an account?

    The minimum deposit to open a Standard Account with Standard FX Trader is US$2,500. Standard FX Trader's minimum transaction size for a Standard Account is US$100,000 (or the equivalent), with a minimum margin deposit of $2,500 (at 100:1 leverage). The minimum deposit to open a Mini Account with Standard FX Trader is US$250. Standard FX Trader's minimum transaction size for a Mini Account is US$10,000 (or the equivalent), with a minimum margin deposit of $50 (at 200:1 leverage).

  • What is FATF?

    The Financial Action Task Force (FATF) is an inter-governmental body whose purpose is the development and promotion of national and international policies to combat money laundering and terrorist financing. The FATF is therefore a 'policy-making body' that was created in 1989 and works to generate the necessary political will to bring about legislative and regulatory reforms in these areas.

  • What happens to my open positions at the end of the trading day?

    Unless specific settlement instructions are provided, Standard FX Trader will automatically roll forward all open positions to the next day's value date at the end of each business day, 23h00 CAT in US summers and at 24h00 CAT in US winters. All rolls will be done at competitive rollover rates. Taking into account the currency pairs involved, trades will be executed where the trader will either earn or pay away points, depending on the interest rate differential between the two currencies and the margin employed on the trade.

    For details see the Trader’s handbook.

  • What is considered a third party?

    Standard FX Trader will not accept third party deposits or withdrawal requests. The name(s) on
    the Standard FX Trader trading account must be an exact match with the name(s) on the account of the other financial institution. This includes (but is not limited to) instances where the name on the bank account is not a Standard FX Trader account holder, business accounts and endorsements to Standard FX Trader.
